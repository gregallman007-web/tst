after birth

Postnatal massage, to help you rest and recover.

The weeks after birth are a strange mix — wonderful, relentless and exhausting all at once. Everyone asks how the baby's getting on. Far fewer people ask how you're doing. A postnatal massage is an hour where the answer to that question actually matters.

£60 · 60 minutes · full-body, Swedish technique

How it helps

Looked after, for once.

Your body has been through a great deal, and the early months of feeding, lifting and carrying ask plenty more of it. A treatment tailored to where you are can help to:

  • ✓Release tension in the neck, shoulders and upper back
  • ✓Ease lower back ache left over from pregnancy and birth
  • ✓Support your body's natural recovery
  • ✓Improve circulation
  • ✓Encourage better sleep, when you can get it
  • ✓Lower stress and give you space to feel like yourself
what it's like

Built around real life with a newborn.

The full-body treatment uses Swedish massage techniques to relax tight muscles, ease tension and get your circulation moving. It runs to 60 minutes and is always adapted to your individual recovery — what your body needs six weeks after a straightforward birth is different from six months on, or after a caesarean.

And if your baby needs you partway through, that's completely fine. We pause, you see to them, and we pick back up.

When can I have one?

After a straightforward birth, once you've had your postnatal check and feel ready, you're generally good to go. After a caesarean or a birth with complications, it's best to wait for your GP or midwife to give the all-clear. Not sure? Just ask — I'm always happy to talk it through first.

A lovely add-on

Hot stones, if you fancy.

Postnatally, you're also welcome to try a hot stone massage — heated stones used through the treatment to ease tension that bit more deeply. A real treat once you're past the pregnancy stage. 50–60 minutes, £75.

Postnatal sessions can be bought on their own or as part of The Blossom Package; any unused pregnancy-package sessions carry over too.
