Take Some Time · Direction 03 locked — logo development

Refining the mark, with the contrast fixed.

You picked Blush & Eucalyptus — so this is where we settle the logo. Four routes below, each shown the way it'd actually be used: primary lockup, reversed, horizontal, and a favicon / social avatar. Every version is on a background it has real contrast against.

Your note was right — blush over the green was muddy. The fix is a deeper eucalyptus for dark backgrounds (so cream and white pop cleanly), with blush kept as a light-background accent only. Here's the before / after:

The contrast fix

Blush (#C99B93) and the light eucalyptus (#8FB1AB) are both mid-tones — side by side they blur together, especially at small sizes.

So on any green panel the wordmark goes cream or white, and blush only ever appears as a small accent (a leaf, a dot, the cross-bar of a letter) on light backgrounds.
